Why local

Built around Ontario build timelines

  • On your calendar

    Parts support, pickup, and order rounds are planned around Ontario competition seasons, school terms, and project deadlines — not a warehouse schedule.

  • One border crossing, not thirty

    Group orders consolidate shipping, customs, and brokerage into a single coordinated round instead of every team paying separately.

  • Nearby when it matters

    Local pickup and handoff around selected events and order rounds. Available by arrangement after inquiry.

  • Real humans who answer

    Run by people from Ontario’s youth robotics community. Ask a question and a person who builds robots replies.

Serving FIRST robotics teams across Ontario — including the Waterloo–Kitchener, Hamilton, and London areas.

Tools for teams & project builders

Send us a request that moves fast

A few practical checklists so your first message has what we need to confirm sourcing, timing, and landed cost — without a dozen back-and-forth emails.

How to send a parts list

  • Vendor and part numbers, or links
  • Quantity for each item
  • Your deadline or event date
  • Whether equivalent substitutes are OK
  • Your city, for local handoff

Start a request

What to include for Everybot BOM support

  • FRC or FTC Everybot, and the season
  • Link or version of the official BOM
  • Team number and event timeline
  • Parts you already have on hand
  • Any Canadian or local-equivalent preferences

Event repair-pack checklist

  • Your robot’s likely failure points
  • Consumables you burn through fastest
  • Pit-side help, or pickup only

Group-order intake checklist
